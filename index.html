About

Mojave Rose is a high-energy modern country band that plays the best of modern and classic country. Mojave Rose is the perfect act to round off (or round up) your event. From rodeos to weddings, from corporate parties to festivals, Mojave Rose has the best mix of cover tunes to get people up and dancing. Mojave Rose features the amazing vocals of Steven Bosco and the beautiful and talented Sarah Ziroll. Together they make up Mojave Rose's front line with heartwarming ballads and powerful duets. Backed-up by a rockin' rhythm section and masterful instrumentation, Mojave Rose always delivers a highly entertaining show.

Vocals, Guitar

Steven Bosco

Steven Lester (or L.) Bosco, Utah born country music singer and songwriter. He began his music career writing traditional country music, heartache style, after a teenage breakup. Steven's sound is classic country with a twist of modern Country Rock.
Steven first gained attention after performing at New York City's renown Club Bonafide and that attention spiraled while performing at the well known Westerner Club, in Salt Lake City. Steven has a single and an EP released and a single being released in 2019, Runs in the Family.
Vocals, Keys

Kevin Brock

Kevin Brock has been singing and tickling the ivory keys for many years. His impressive range and ability to jam out on the piano add dimension and depth to Mojave Rose. As a Utah graduate, Kevin sings with pride, "A Utah Man Am I!". Kevin plays with numerous groups in the valley, and is a lover of all genres of music
Vocals, Fiddle

Sarah Ziroll

Sarah is a master violinist and vocalist whose music has enchanted audiences around the world. Not just a pretty face, Sarah is a triple threat. She started playing Violin at the age of 5 and holds a masters degree in violin performance from the University of Utah. Sarah has performed on major motion picture sound tracks and orchestral ensembles. Sarah also sings and writes music and teaches violin.
Guitar, Vocals

Jeff Hubbard

Jeff taught himself to play guitar at a young age and started playing in high school bands with his buddy Rich Bonaduce. After school, Jeff experimented with many different genres of music; but ultimately his love of Country music prevails. He has played with various bands in Utah, including: Swim Herschel Swim, Rawhide, The Jim Huish Band, and Jackwagon, before finding a home with Mojave Rose.
Drums

Rich Bonaduce

Rich began playing on household objects years before his parents finally relented and bought him a snow-white Tama Swingstar, and he hasn't been far from a kit, since. Born in Pennsylvania, he didn't play in a "proper" band until he moved to southern Utah playing both kinds of music: country and western!
After graduating SUU, Rich has worked in television in Salt Lake City, currently being the Film Critic and Entertainment Reporter for FOX13, KSTU. In between seeing movies and interviewing celebrities, Rich played drums in various bands such as Rawhide, the Jim Huish Band, Jackwagon and Buzzy Bee Blues.
When not hitting things with sticks, Rich can be found indoors catching a movie, or outdoors catching some rays!
Bass

Joe Dobesh

Joe first developed a love for country as a small child watching Roy Clark tearing it up on his banjo on Hee Haw. He started playing bass guitar in high school and mostly played in rock bands, but never lost that place in his heart for pickin' and a grinnin'. Once out of high school, he expanded his talents in blues and country and played in a variety of bands around Minnesota. After moving to Utah in 2000, Joe played in many country bands in the valley, including Monrovia, Rawhide, MoDixy, and Rockwell Station. Along with his other half, Jeff on drums, Joe lays down solid rhythms that drive the unique sound which is Mojave Rose. Joe has a degree in engineering and when he is not playing, he loves the outdoors, traveling and hanging with his children
