Mojave Rose is a high-energy modern country band that plays the best of modern and classic country. Mojave Rose is the perfect act to round off (or round up) your event. From rodeos to weddings, from corporate parties to festivals, Mojave Rose has the best mix of cover tunes to get people up and dancing. Mojave Rose features the amazing baritone vocals of Michael Kendal Harris complimented with versatile tenor vocals of Anthony Penrose and the beautiful and talented Sarah Ziroll. Together they make up Mojave Rose's front line with heartwarming ballads and powerful duets. Backed-up by a rockin' rhythm section and masterful instrumentation, Mojave Rose always delivers a highly entertaining show.

About

Vocals, Guitar

Tony Penrose

Tony started playing music when he found a guitar in his dad's garage at 13. Since then he has played in downtown Nashville, Vegas, California, and most of the west coast. Tony has won multiple karaoke competitions over the past 15 years including 2nd place in a national singing competition in Laughlin, Nevada. He has been playing solo shows around Utah for the last 2 years. Most notable is playing with members of Tim McGraw's band.
Vocals, Keys

Michael Kendal Harris

Michael has been singing and playing the piano, in some capacity, for audiences since he was four years old. Michael has been totally blind his entire life due to a genetic birth defect. Therefore, he uses hearing as his primary sense. Some feel that it is because of this, that Michael has an uncanny ability to nearly duplicate the sound of any singer in his two and a half octave vocal range. It is this talent that has helped him win many awards and accolades in the music field throughout his life, including the national male country talent quest, Karaoke champion in 2004, and the male pop title in the same contest in 2006.
Vocals, Fiddle

Sarah Ziroll

Sarah is a master violinist and vocalist whose music has enchanted audiences around the world. Not just a pretty face, Sarah is a triple threat. She started playing Violin at the age of 5 and holds a masters degree in violin performance from the University of Utah. Sarah has performed on major motion picture sound tracks and orchestral ensembles. Sarah also sings and writes music and teaches violin through her website
Vocals, Guitar

John Clark

Originally from Tucson Arizona, Johnny Plays guitar and manages Mojave Rose. He is a graduate of the University of Utah music program with a double major in composition and jazz performance. John recieved the Presidents Award and was place on the Dean's list for the music department and was a member of the University Jazz Guitar Ensemble. John is an application engineer Harman International (currently the largest audio company in the world) and is involved daily with audio system design and integration and support for BSS Audio and IDX systems. At Harman, John is involved in audio design and works on many high profile audio systems including NFL arena's, Las Vegas casinos, international airports and large theme parks. In his spare time he composes music in his home studio, works on computers, and of course playing guitar.


Vocals, Drums

Jeff Walpole

Jeff has been playing drums and percussion since he was 9 years old. A university music scholarship allowed Jeff to play in a variety of musical settings, including symphony orchestras, marching bands and jazz ensembles.  This also secured him multiple drum students and a performance seat in the music industry. This part-time position has evolved into 35 years of recording, touring and performing in multiple bands, including The Keith Gilley Band, The Dudes, Gypsy Train, Tabu (a Santana Tribute band), Artie Hemphill &The Iron Horse Band, and players from the Steve Miller Band among many other acts.
Bass

Joseph Dobesh

Joe first developed a love for country as a small child watching Roy Clark tearing it up on his banjo on Hee Haw. He started playing bass guitar in high school and mostly played in rock bands, but never lost that place in his heart for pickin' and a grinnin'. Once out of high school, he expanded his talents in blues and country and played in a variety of bands around Minnesota. After moving to Utah in 2000, Joe played in many country bands in the valley, including Monrovia, Rawhide, MoDixy, and Rockwell Station. Along with his other half, Jeff on drums, Joe lays down solid rhythms that drive the unique sound which is Mojave Rose. Joe has a degree in engineering and when he is not playing, he loves the outdoors, traveling and hanging with his children


Mojave Rose plays a variety of music besides country. Our list includes current top 40 dance hits as well as rock classics, modern and classic country. We can also do a low volume acoustic or jazz dinner, cocktail hour or jazz sets upon request as well.
